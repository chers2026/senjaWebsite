Water confidence

8 GOLDEN RULES

No child should have access to the pool or facility without adult supervision. Children are taught not to go swimming without an adult but it only takes a few steps or a slip on poolside for an accident to happen so it is paramount that that they have the core swimming skills to ensure safety.

Eight core skills that need to be to ensure a child’s safety in the water :

  1. Entries and exits
  2. Buoyancy and balance
  3. Travel and co-ordination
  4. Streamlining
  5. Rotation and orientation
  6. Aquatic breathing
  7. Water safety
  8. Health and fitness.

It is common to find parents who will stop the swimming journey when they feel their children are safe or able to swim 50m, but their children still has not developed enough skills and strength to be really good swimmers at that stage.

It is of utmost importance for your toddler or pre-schooler to learn water safety rules and about boundaries especially if they enjoy aquatics. Enhance your child's interest for the aquatic environment and teach them a skill that may one day save their life. The earlier you start the better!

At Senja Cashew SwimSafer , we understand the importance of making your child feel comfortable with our swim teachers in the water – generally, when children enter our Toddlers’ programme, it is their first time swimming in the water without the security of Mum and Dad. Programmes

Swimming Classes FOR KIDS (From 4 -16 years old)

This programme is aimed at swimmers that understand the key FUNdamentals and core aquatic skills. It would be a perfect transition from the foundation classes, focusing on your child’s progress in the water, developing a solid groundwork for all swim strokes and competitive skills.

Our lessons will be mainly focused on freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke and butterfly strokes. It gives swimmers their first insight into competitive swimming and is the stepping stone for swimmers to transition into any Squad Training Programme, to become the next Olympics Champion or to being featured in Aquaman sequels.

Our development plan paves ways to improve on necessary skills, in and out of the pool to enable swimmers of all abilities to fulfil their long-term potentials. The lesson plans are delivered according to each child’s capability to allow swimmers to develop in a fun, safe, team oriented, learning environment. These lessons are extremely beneficial for children over the age of four as it enhances their knowledge of water survival / safety around water as well furthering their skills and stroke development.
